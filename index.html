Road to Euro 2028 · Stage 1 · League phase · Finals

Nations League Finals

The eight best teams in Europe. Two-legged quarter-finals in March 2027, then a four-team finals tournament in June — host to be decided.

Quarter-finals: group winners are seeded and host the second leg; teams from the same group cannot meet. Ties level on aggregate go to extra time, then penalties (shootouts count as draws for Elo). Host selection: UEFA requires two Category 4 stadiums of 30,000+ (some flexibility on the second), ideally within 150 km; each finalist's two designated stadiums are pre-vetted below. The host's larger stadium stages their semi-final and the final.

Road to Euro 2028 · Stage 1 · League phase · Play-offs

Promotion & relegation play-offs

Two-legged ties in March 2027, played alongside the finals quarter-finals. League A's third-placed sides defend their place against League B's runners-up; League B's third-placed sides face League C's runners-up. Winners take the higher league's place in 2027-28.

Higher-league sides are seeded and host the second leg. Ties level on aggregate go to extra time then penalties (shootouts count as draws for Elo). The C/D relegation play-offs — the two best fourth-placed League C sides against the League D runners-up — are not played until March 2028 and unlock after the Euro qualification stage.

Road to Euro 2028 · Stage 2 · Qualifying draw

Euro 2028 Qualifying

Twelve groups — A–F with four teams, G–L with five. Pots follow the Nations League ranking directly (which automatically places the eight NL quarter-finalists in Pot 1). Five-team groups start in March 2027, so each may contain at most one of the 24 teams playing Nations League football that month — quarter-finalists and play-off sides (tagged Mar NL) — and the NL semi-finalists, busy until June, go into the four-team groups. Hosts are kept apart and UEFA's political, winter-venue and travel restrictions apply.

Constraints: one team per pot per group (pot 5 only in groups G–L) · hosts England, Scotland, Wales & Rep. Ireland kept apart · Nations League semi-finalists in groups A–F · max one March-2027 Nations League participant (quarter-finalists and promotion/relegation play-off sides) per five-team group · prohibited pairings Serbia/Kosovo, Bosnia & Herzegovina/Kosovo, Spain/Gibraltar, Armenia/Azerbaijan, Ukraine/Belarus · max two winter-venue nations (Belarus, Estonia, Faroe Islands, Finland, Iceland, Latvia, Lithuania, Norway) per group · at most one long-haul western opponent for Kazakhstan. Seeding by NL ranking makes UEFA's Pot-1 quarter-finalist rule and pot-swap contingency automatically satisfied.

Road to Euro 2028 · Stage 2 · Play-offs

Euro 2028 Play-offs

March 2028. The remaining berths, contested by the worst qualifying runners-up and the best non-qualified Nations League group winners. The format flexes with how many host berths were used.

Seeding: for path draws, entrants are ranked runners-up first (by European Qualifiers ranking) then Nations League qualifiers (by interim NL ranking), split into four pots; each path pairs Pot 1 v Pot 4 and Pot 2 v Pot 3, higher-ranked team hosts each single-leg match, better-ranked finalist hosts the final. For four home-and-away ties, Pot 1 (runners-up) v Pot 2 (NL qualifiers). Two host teams are kept in separate paths/ties; Northern Ireland avoids a host where possible.

Road to Euro 2028 · Stage 2 · Qualified teams

Euro 2028 Qualified

All 24 teams for the final tournament, sorted into the four pots for the finals draw. Pot 1 holds the qualified hosts; the remaining non-play-off teams follow by their European Qualifiers ranking; the play-off winners fill Pot 4.

The finals group draw is the next stage.

Seeding for the final tournament draw: Pot 1 = the host nations that qualified, then the highest-ranked qualified teams by European Qualifiers ranking; Pots 2 and 3 continue down that ranking; Pot 4 is completed by the play-off winners. Hosts are placed in Pot 1 regardless of ranking.
